Data set: Bird Cognition Data

Danae Diaz (another grad student in our department) spent the summer gathering data to test her hypothesis that metal contamination affects bird cognition (Im simplifying her actual experiment herefeel free to reach out to her to learn the full story). She used puzzle boxes at four field sites with varying levels of metal contamination and measured how long it took the birds to complete the puzzle (again, Im simplifying things). She re-ran the puzzle 6 times over one day for each bird and recorded the following variables:

  • The date on which she ran the experiment: Date

  • The sex of the bird: Sex

  • The time it took the birds to complete the puzzle (in minutes) for each trial: Trial 0 X0.5 Trial 1 X1Trial 2 X2Trial 3 X3Trial 4 X4 and Trial 5 X5

  • A two-letter abbreviation for the field site, and the number designation for the puzzle box: SiteBox

She also recorded in a separate data sheet Site contamination:

  • The full name for each field site: SiteName

  • The matching two letter abbreviation she used in the bird cognition data set: ID

  • A ranking of the level of contamination, where 1 was the most contaminated site and 4 was the least: Contamination Level (1=most)

Please note that the values included here are NOT the values from Danaes actual experimentthey have been manipulated to allow us to practice the skills weve learned in this class. Nevertheless, some of the wrangling problems you will encounter are true to the original data sheet, and others are extremely common issues you will repeatedly encounter throughout your career.

Looking Ahead:

In this problem set, you will be asked to pull together the skills you have learned so far in this course to answer the following questions:

  • Is there a sex difference in how quickly the birds complete the puzzle?

  • Does metal contamination seem to affect how quickly the birds complete the puzzle?

  • Is there co-variation between sex-differences and field site differences?

  • Does the time-of-year in which the trial was run seem to impact how quickly the birds completed the puzzle? (i.e.did birds complete the puzzle quicker at the beginning of the summer).

  • Is there any difference in the rate at which the birds learned to solve the puzzle over each trial by sex or by field site?

To answer these questions, we will need to split data by both position and by a deliminator, we will need to pivot the data from wide to long, we will need to join two data sets, and we will need to coerce columns into different data types (and more!)
